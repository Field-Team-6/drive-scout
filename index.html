🎯 Drive Scout FAQ

What is Drive Scout?
Drive Scout helps progressive activists find the best college campuses for voter drives — in the 63 most hotly-contested House districts for 2026! Drive Scout is a project of Field Team 6, the one and only national organization laser-focused on registering Democrats in swing districts & states, all year, every year.
Do I need to get permission from a college before holding a voter drive?
You do not! Voter registration is constitutionally protected activity — and a public service. Usually, all is good. Rarely, they ask us to leave, which we do politely, and go find another campus.
Can I hold a voter drive indoors?
Not without permission.
What do the drive ratings mean?
HIGH Perfect for drives! Large enrollment (10,000+), public institutions with significant campus space.

MEDIUM Good opportunity. Mid-sized schools (3,000-10,000) or smaller public colleges.

LOW More challenging. Smaller schools (<3,000) or specialized institutions. Still worth considering if convenient!
How do you calculate the drive ratings?
We factor in 1) enrollment, 2) campus size, 3) type of school, and 4) open quad areas.
Is Drive Scout affiliated with the Democratic Party?
It is not. Drive Scout is a project of Field Team 6, a proudly partisan voter registration organization. Our legal designation is a 527 federal PAC working on the IE side.
What if my closest swing district is far away?
You can still help! Consider: (1) Organizing drives when you visit the area, (2) Supporting Field Team 6 efforts by donating, or (3) Textbanking or writing postcards from home.
Why do you sometimes list colleges outside a swing district?
Often if a college is close to a district, many students actually live in a neighboring district.
